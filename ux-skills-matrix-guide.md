## First, skill self-assessment
## 第一步：技能自我評估

> Your Design Skills Matrix should have all sticky notes placed on the grid.
>
> 你的設計技能矩陣應該要將所有便利貼都放到格子上。

Some skills listed are things you haven't learned yet. Those skills should be aligned to the left side of the grid, but if you want to learn more about them, move them upward into the "Want to do more" area.

部分列出的技能是你尚未學習的。這些技能應該對齊格子的左側，但如果你想多了解它們，可以將它們向上移到「想做更多」的區域。

It's okay if some skills overlap. There is no "perfect placement."
It's more important to **make sure you can read each of the skill labels.**

技能之間有重疊是沒關係的。並不存在「完美的放置位置」。
更重要的是 **確保你能清楚看到每個技能標籤的文字。**

## Axis Definitions — 兩條軸線定義

### Want To Do More
### 想做更多（縱軸）

These are skills you want to do more of in an ideal world. Don't let the politics of your current workplace influence your placement on this axis.

這些是你在理想狀態下想更多發揮的技能。不要讓目前職場的政治因素影響你在這條軸上的判斷。

---

### Expert At This
### 專業程度（橫軸）

Think of this right-hand side of the matrix as a continuum with 4 sections.
Just to the right of the vertical center line is **"I'm pretty good at this"** and all the way at the right side of the matrix is **"I could teach other people how to do this."**

把矩陣右半邊想成一個連續的區間，共分為 4 個段落。
稍微超過中線的位置代表 **「我還算擅長」**，最右側代表 **「我可以教別人怎麼做」**。

**Expertise Level Definitions / 專業等級定義：**

| Level | English | 中文 |
|---|---|---|
| 還算擅長 | If you're "pretty good" at this, you've done it successfully a few times, maybe with supervision or help. | 若你「還算擅長」這項技能，代表你曾成功完成幾次，可能是在監督或協助下完成的。 |
| 非常擅長 | If you're "really good" at this, you've done it successfully at least 7–8 times, with limited supervision or help. | 若你「非常擅長」，代表你曾成功完成至少 7–8 次，且幾乎不需要監督或幫助。 |
| 相當出色 | If you're "amazing" at this, you've done it professionally at least 12 times and can clearly explain how you got results. | 若你「相當出色」，代表你曾以專業水準完成至少 12 次，並能清楚說明如何取得成果。 |
| 領域專家 | If you're "an expert" at this, you've done it successfully at least 20 times, and have taught others how to do it well. | 若你是「領域專家」，代表你曾成功完成至少 20 次，且曾教導過他人如何做到。 |

## Second, interpreting your matrix
## 第二步：解讀你的矩陣

See any patterns or clusters of color?

你有看到任何顏色的規律或聚落嗎？

You may notice that patterns or clusters of certain colors begin to emerge in the top right quadrant. These are **areas of UX/UI design you may want to specialize in.**

你可能會注意到特定顏色的規律或聚落開始在右上象限浮現。這些就是 **你可能想要深入發展的 UX/UI 設計專業方向。**

---

### The Four Quadrants / 四個象限

#### Top Left Quadrant — "I like this, but I don't know enough yet."
#### 左上象限 ——「我喜歡，但我還不夠了解」

The skills in the **top left quadrant** that fall outside of your sweet spot, are the ones you can prioritize for learning and growing later.

**左上象限**中位於甜蜜點之外的技能，是你日後可以優先學習與成長的方向。

If you see these skills on job descriptions, you will be able to speak to your existing knowledge in interviews and demonstrate excitement to learn more! These might also indicate how well a role will fit you, when looking at more than one job description.

若你在職缺描述中看到這些技能，可以在面試中展現你現有的基礎知識，並表達想進一步學習的熱情！在比較多份職缺描述時，這些也可以作為判斷一份工作是否適合你的參考依據。

---

#### Top Right Quadrant (Sweet Spot) — "I'm great at these things, and I would almost do them for free."
#### 右上象限（甜蜜點）——「我很擅長這些，而且幾乎願意免費做」

Any skills that fall within the **'sweet spot'** are the most important ones you want to continue to use as a primary part of your work. These skills are the ones you should focus on growing more familiar and proficient with.

落在 **「甜蜜點」** 範圍內的技能，是你最想持續作為工作核心的重要技能。這些技能是你應該專注深化、持續精進的方向。

**Look for your 'sweet spot' keywords, and phrases like them, in job descriptions** to find work that is likely to feel challenging but exciting.

**在職缺描述中尋找你「甜蜜點」的關鍵字及類似詞彙**，以找到可能令你感到有挑戰性又興奮的工作。

Any skills that fall within the **'cherry on top'** are your absolute best skills. You enjoy doing these things and you could help others learn how to do them, too. You might even consider volunteering these skills for a project you're passionate about!

落在 **「錦上添花」** 區域的技能是你絕對最強的技能。你享受做這些事，也能幫助他人學習。你甚至可以考慮在你熱愛的專案中貢獻這些技能！

**Look for these keywords, and phrases like them, in job descriptions** to find work that is likely to feel meaningful and optimized for your particular skillset.

**在職缺描述中尋找這些關鍵字及類似詞彙**，以找到可能讓你深感有意義、且最能發揮你技能組合的工作。

---

#### Bottom Left Quadrant — "I don't like this and I don't care."
#### 左下象限 ——「我不喜歡這個，而且也不在乎」

The skills in the **bottom left quadrant** that fall outside of your sweet spot, are the ones you really don't enjoy, don't make you feel successful and are not job requirements you want to gain experience with.

**左下象限**中位於甜蜜點之外的技能，是你真的不享受、不讓你感到有成就感、也不是你想獲得更多經驗的工作要求。

Watch out for these on job descriptions and in interviews, make sure you're playing to your strengths!

在職缺描述和面試中要留意這些技能，確保你在發揮自己的強項！

---

#### Bottom Right Quadrant — "I'm good at this and I will do it if I have to."
#### 右下象限 ——「我做得來，必要時我會去做」

The skills in the **bottom right quadrant** that fall outside of your sweet spot, are the ones you could do in your sleep or without much effort, however doing these skills does not bring you joy or satisfaction. If needed, on occasion, those are skills you can rely on to support your team.

**右下象限**中位於甜蜜點之外的技能，是你輕而易舉就能完成的事，但做這些技能並不帶給你喜悅或滿足感。若有需要，這些偶爾可以作為支援團隊的技能。

Keep an eye out for these skills in job descriptions and in interviews — highlighting that you have these skills might be a way to stand out from the crowd and differentiate you from a close competitor for a role.

在職缺描述和面試中留意這些技能——強調你擁有這些技能，可能是讓你從眾多候選人中脫穎而出、與競爭對手產生差異的方式。

---

### Watch out for your "Danger Zone"
### 注意你的「危險地帶」

The skills in the **top left quadrant** that fall on the left edge of your sweet spot are the ones you'll need to watch out for. These skills are exciting enough that they will distract you from your day-to-day job duties or even prevent you from moving your top skills from good to great (becoming more proficient).

**左上象限**中落在甜蜜點左緣的技能，是你需要特別留意的。這些技能令你興奮，足以讓你分心於日常工作職責，甚至阻礙你將核心技能從「好」提升到「更好」（持續精進）。

If you have ADHD or struggle to focus on the important things (rather than the fun things), spending time increasing these skills will keep you busy, but probably won't give a good return on your time investment unless you're mindful about why and how you approach your learning. To be successful, first **create a strategy, then execute on your plan.**

如果你有 ADHD 或難以專注在重要事項（而非有趣的事）上，花時間提升這些技能會讓你保持忙碌，但除非你清楚自己為何學習、如何學習，否則這可能不會帶來好的時間回報。要成功，首先要 **制定策略，再執行計畫。**

---

### Double Check Your Expertise
### 再次確認你的專業程度

Take a harder look at the things you added to the top right quadrant. Pay particular attention to the notes for each sub-quadrant. Do you meet that criteria or do you need to shuffle anything around?

仔細重新審視你放到右上象限的技能。特別留意每個子象限的標準說明。你是否真的符合那些標準，還是需要調整一些位置？

Many early-career designers lack context for how their skills measure up in the real world. They get overconfident about what they're capable of based on their knowledge of how other students did in the bootcamp they attended. Lots of UX/UI Bootcamp students will place skills in this area, but get a rude awakening when it comes time to perform these activities and methods as part of a real job.

許多職涯初期的設計師缺乏對自身技能在真實職場中水準的認知。他們往往因為在所參加的訓練營中與其他學員的比較，而過度高估自己的能力。許多 UX/UI 訓練營的學生會將技能放在這個區域，但真正進入職場、需要在工作中執行這些活動與方法時，才意識到現實的落差。

---

## Why is this my sweet spot?
## 為什麼這是我的甜蜜點？

While it would be *amazing* to only work with the skills you **want to do more**, the reality is that it's impossible to get a job ONLY doing things you're awesome at. What's more, only polishing your best skills makes you a less flexible, well-rounded designer!

雖然只發揮你 **想做更多** 的技能聽起來很理想，但現實是，你不可能找到一份工作只做你最擅長的事。而且，只磨練你的最強技能，反而會讓你成為一個彈性較低、缺乏全面性的設計師！

## Third, focus on your sweet spot
## 第三步：聚焦你的甜蜜點

### What to do next with this information
### 接下來怎麼運用這些資訊

1. Highlight your top skills on your portfolio case studies.
   在作品集案例中突顯你的核心技能。

2. Incorporate these into your personal brand statement, website intro blurb, LinkedIn bio, etc.
   將這些融入你的個人品牌聲明、網站介紹、LinkedIn 個人簡介等。

3. Include these keywords on your portfolio, resume and cover letter.
   在作品集、履歷和求職信中納入這些關鍵字。

4. Add these as primary skills on your LinkedIn profile (and request endorsements!).
   在 LinkedIn 上將這些列為主要技能（並請求他人背書！）。

5. Use these words and phrases as prompts for requesting recommendations from previous coworkers and teammates.
   用這些詞彙作為請求前同事或隊友撰寫推薦的提示。

6. Work these skills into your elevator pitch.
   在你的電梯簡報中融入這些技能。

7. Mention these skills when answering STAR behavioral interview questions.
   在回答 STAR 行為面試問題時提及這些技能。

8. Align your design challenge deliverables for job interviews to highlight the skills you know are your strongest.
   在求職面試的設計挑戰題成果中，對齊並突顯你已知的最強技能。
